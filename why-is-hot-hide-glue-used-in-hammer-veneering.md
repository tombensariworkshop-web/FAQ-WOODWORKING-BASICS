# Why Is Hot Hide Glue Used in Hammer Veneering

Hot hide glue has properties that make it particularly suitable for traditional veneering techniques.

When heated, hide glue becomes fluid and spreads easily across the surface. As it cools, the glue begins to gel and gradually hardens.

This change in viscosity allows the veneer to be positioned and pressed into place with a veneer hammer while excess glue is pushed outward.

Another advantage of hide glue is reversibility. Heat and moisture can soften the adhesive again, which makes repairs and adjustments possible.
