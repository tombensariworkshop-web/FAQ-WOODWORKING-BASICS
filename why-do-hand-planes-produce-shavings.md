# Why Do Hand Planes Produce Shavings

Hand planes produce shavings because the blade slices wood fibers and lifts them from the surface in a continuous strip.

When the blade is sharp and properly set, the shaving curls as it passes through the mouth of the plane.

The thickness of the shaving depends on blade projection, grain direction and cutting angle.
