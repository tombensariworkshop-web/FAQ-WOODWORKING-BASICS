# What Is Grain in Wood

Grain describes the direction and pattern of wood fibers within a piece of timber.

Because wood fibers run primarily along the length of the tree, their orientation affects how tools cut the material and how the wood behaves during drying and seasonal humidity changes.

Woodworkers often examine grain patterns before cutting or planing a board, because grain direction strongly influences surface quality.
