# What Is Interlocked Grain

Interlocked grain occurs when wood fibers change direction repeatedly as the tree grows.

This structure can make woodworking more difficult because the grain direction alternates across the board.

When planing interlocked grain, cutting tools may encounter fibers running in different directions, which increases the risk of tear-out.
